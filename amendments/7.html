Amendment VII

Jury Trial in Civil Lawsuits


In Suits at common law, where the value in controversy shall exceed twenty dollars, the right of trial by jury shall be preserved, and no fact tried by a jury, shall be otherwise reexamined in any Court of the United States, than according to the rules of the common law.

Passed by Congress September 25, 1789. Ratified December 15, 1791. Part of the first 10 amendments from the Bill of Rights

The Seventh Amendment would guarantee Americans to have the right to trial with a jury with any case involving greater than twenty dollars. Americans have the right to choose to have a jury of our peers or have just a judge to decide the case. One recurring problem with this ammendment was what the definition of "common law" would be, leaving people to have to assume what the proposers ment by this and what would be violating the Seventh Amendment.
