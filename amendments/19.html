Amendment XIX

The Right for Women Vote


Section 1
The right of citizens of the United States to vote shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any State on account of sex.
Section 2
Congress shall have power to enforce this article by appropriate legislation.

Passed by Congress June 4, 1919. Ratified August 18, 1920

Women for the whole time, until adoption of the Nineteenth Amendment, have not gotten the proper right to vote. The Nineteenth Amendment allows the right for women to have a say in the vote of anything. Many attempts to vote and protests to vote led to congress to make the decision to let women have a say in vote.
