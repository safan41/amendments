Amendment XVI

Income Tax


The Congress shall have power to lay and collect taxes on incomes, from whatever source derived, without apportionment among the several States, and without regard to any census or enumeration.

Passed by Congress July 2, 1909. Ratified February 3, 1913.

Before the radification of the Sixteenth Amendment, taxes were highly based on the populaiton of states (direct taxes) and trading of goods (indirect taxes), layed out by Article I, Section 9. This ruled to be unreasonable as people with no source of income wouldn't be able to pay their taxes. The Sixteenth Amendment rules out the direct tax protocol by instating an national income tax, tax directly based off of each individual's income.
