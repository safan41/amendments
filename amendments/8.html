Amendment 8

Rights for Fair Punishments


Excessive bail shall not be required, nor excessive fines imposed, nor cruel and unusual punishments inflicted.

Passed by Congress September 25, 1789. Ratified December 15, 1791. Part of the first 10 amendments from the Bill of Rights

As previous documents have ensured no unjust punishments to poeple, composers of the Constitution felt the need to ensure this. The Eighth Amendment simply protects from having to do three specific things in trial:

  • Having to pay excessive bail
    • This means you don't need to unjustly pay more than what the case was worth to get out of prison.
  • Having to pay excessive fines
  • Having to endure "cruel and unusual punishments"
    • Anything inhumane, such as torturing someone who was stealing, would be cruel.
