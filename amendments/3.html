Amendment III

Quartering of Soldiers


No Soldier shall, in time of peace be quartered in any house, without the consent of the Owner, nor in time of war, but in a manner to be prescribed by law.

Passed by Congress September 25, 1789. Ratified December 15, 1791. Part of the first 10 amendments from the Bill of Rights

The Third Amendment prevents soldiers to stay under private houses as long as the owner or law permits them. The need for this was really to go against the British Quartering Act which enforced soldier to be able to stay in private homes without consent of owner. Nowadays, the importance of this amendment isn't to great, but it shows regulation that was needed when the country was created.
