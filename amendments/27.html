Amendment XXVII

Congressional Pay Raises


No law, varying the compensation for the services of the Senators and Representatives, shall take effect, until an election of representatives shall have intervened.

Originally proposed September 25, 1789. Ratified May 7, 1992

The Twenty-Seventh Amendment actually was proposed along with the Bill of Rights, but it was halted due to people finding it would've given Congress memebers too much pay. Only 6 states ratified this, so nothing really happened until the 1980s where people disapproved of salaries meeting an abundance. The Twenty-Seventh Ammendent agreed that all congress members can have pay raises after "an election of representatives" came in to play.
