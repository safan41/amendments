Amendment XXIV

Aboltition of Poll Taxes


Section 1
The right of citizens of the United States to vote in any primary or other election for President or Vice President, for electors for President or Vice President, or for Senator or Representative in Congress, shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or any State by reason of failure to pay poll tax or other tax.
Section 2
The Congress shall have power to enforce this article by appropriate legislation.

Passed by Congress August 27, 1962. Ratified January 23, 1964

Many states had required a tax to vote, espcially black voters. The Twenty-Fourth Amendment abolishes the use of a poll tax. Many farmers for a long time found this necessary to pour accurate results. Poll tax in many states was enforced heavily on black voters as people wanted to make the ballot process more and more difficult for them.
