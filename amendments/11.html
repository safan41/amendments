Amendment XI

Suits against States


The Judicial power of the United States shall not be construed to extend to any suit in law or equity, commenced or prosecuted against one of the United States by Citizens of another State, or by Citizens or Subjects of any Foreign State.

Passed by Congress March 4, 1794. Ratified February 7, 1795.

Before the Eleventh Amendment was ratified, many people have been seen to abuse Article III, Section 2, which allowed people to sue other states without leaving in them in federal court. The Eleventh Amendment would prevent federal courts to have to hear lawsuits against states, leaving the states to figure it out unless it is decided by state courts to go to federal level.
