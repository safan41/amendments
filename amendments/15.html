Amendment XV

Right to Vote Not Denied by Race


Section 1
The right of citizens of the United States to vote shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any State on account of race, color, or previous condition of servitude.
Section 2
The Congress shall have the power to enforce this article by appropriate legislation.

Passed by Congress February 26, 1869. Ratified February 3, 1870

Before the Civil Rights Movement that would've taken place a century after ratification, there were already laws that allowed African Americans to vote. The Fifiteenth Amendment required all accounts of voting to be available to any citizen of the United States, regardless of race. The purpose of this was to unify the standard of voting, yet African Americans couldn't really vote at the time of the 1960s because states like Alabama just made it harder for African Americans to vote with unjust confirmation processes rather than to outright deny a ballot.
