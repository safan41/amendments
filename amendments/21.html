Amendment XXI

Repeal of Prohibition


Section 1
The eighteenth article of amendment to the Constitution of the United States is hereby repealed.
Section 2
The transportation or importation into any State, Territory, or Possession of the United States for delivery or use therein of intoxicating liquors, in violation of the laws thereof, is hereby prohibited.
Section 3
This article shall be inoperative unless it shall have been ratified as an amendment to the Constitution by conventions in the several States, as provided in the Constitution, within seven years from the date of the submission hereof to the States by the Congress.

Passed by Congress February 20, 1933. Ratified December 5, 1933.

Seeing people ignoring the purpose of the Eighteenth Amendment and to see increased problems is what caused Congress to repeal the Eighteenth Amendment. The crime rate was much higher than Congress thought it would be if enacted. The main cause of this was simply people's love with liquor, and feel restricted if the right to have liquor is gone from them. People started to secretly sell liquor, which eventually led to to more and more government disapprovement.
