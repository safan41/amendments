Amendment X

The Rights Reserved to the States or People


The powers not delegated to the United States by the Constitution, nor prohibited by it to the States, are reserved to the States respectively, or to the people.

Passed by Congress September 25, 1789. Ratified December 15, 1791. Part of the first 10 amendments from the Bill of Rights

Drafters of the Bill of Rights did think that other rights not included in the Constitution can be protected to the people, but to have security, they gave power to control and influence people over state plans. The Tenth and final Amendment from the Bill of Rights gives all powers not listed in the constitution to the states and its people. This means things such as:

  • Education
  • Hospitals
  • Police
and others that are dependant on state law.
